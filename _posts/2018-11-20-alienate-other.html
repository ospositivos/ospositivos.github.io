À vossa consideração então. 1) Colocando-vos uma questão para hipótese de trabalho:

A fairly fundamental question: why post content for free to Facebook, who then sells ads and makes money that they don’t share with him, and which further charges him money to actually deliver his posts that might make him money so he can afford to keep making the content they’re monetizing? Why should anybody?
in "The First Thing We Do: Let’s Alienate All The Content Makers" 19 nov 2018

2) Um pequeno recap em state-of-the-art como chegámos aqui, o perigo evidente, e a solução previdente.

There’s been a major shift away from webcomics folk maintaining their own sites in the past few years, with Tumblr and various portal-type sites (Taptastic, Webtoon) offering free hosting and eyeballs that might not have landed on an individual site in this mostly post-RSS (and bookmarkless) world. But any time you rely on somebody else’s infrastructure to run your business/art/lifestyle/whatever, you run the possibility of it being taken away by somebody whose priorities are not yours. So use other (free) services all you like, but keep your content someplace besides the free service. For the sake of your work and my peace of mind, keep a copy someplace so you can rebuild when the free service du jour decides you don’t get to use them for free (or at all) after today.
in "The First Thing We Do: Let’s Alienate All The Content Makers" 19 nov 2018
