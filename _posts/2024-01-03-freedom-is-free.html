Retornamos à Indieweb, catch-all de tecnologias e condutas entre os que insistem em comunicar online sem gatekeepers ou aputalhar a sua identidade digital por terceiros. No seguimento do nosso rambling de fim-de-ano chegou-nos um artigo da Rolling Stone com promessas de algo que foi obliterado no último quarto de século: "The Internet Is About to Get Weird Again" 30 dez 2023.

millions of people seem poised to connect with each other in new ways, as they reconsider their fundamental relationship to technology

We are about to see the biggest reshuffling of power on the internet in 25 years, in a way that most of the internet's current users have never seen before. Some of the most dominant companies on the internet are at risk of losing their relevance, and the rest of us are rethinking our daily habits in ways that will shift the digital landscape as we know it. Amidst it all, the human web, the one made by regular people, is resurgent. We're seeing the biggest return to that human-run, personal-scale web that we've witnessed since the turn of the millennium.
in "The Internet Is About to Get Weird Again" 30 dez 2023

Batem a tecla do declínio do Twitter e substituição por plataformas das outras grandes, tão-quais os carcereiros da web, ie, o velho aliciar dos utilizadores com goodies antes de fechar porta do curral atrás. Porém mencionam o impacto dos reguladores para as abrir, permitindo as alternativas que serão uma bonança especialmente satisfatória aos criadores. Deste exercício aquele que nos traz mais lágrimas aos olhos: a memória do back than, a década mágica de todas as coisas web/zine,

Back then, a lot of technology was created by local communities or people with a shared interest, and it was as likely that cool things would be invented by universities and non-profits and eccentric lone creators as they were to be made by giant corporations. Going back to the more free-for-all nature of the Nineties internet could mean we see a proliferation of unexpected, strange new products and services. In that era, people could even make their own little social networks, so the conversations and content you found on an online forum or discussion were as likely to have been hosted by the efforts of one lone creator than to have come from some giant corporate conglomerate. It was a more democratized internet, and while the world can't return to that level of simplicity, we're seeing signs of a modern revisiting of some of those ideas.
in "The Internet Is About to Get Weird Again" 30 dez 2023

Porém. Controlem o vosso optimismo, todos os fins/inicios de ano publicam-se (nos grandes conglomerados de media) textos nostálgicos de tempos mais simples. Revejam arquivos nos P+ e não faltará literatura recolhida. Olh-ó The Guardian, dias antes:

Blogging, for me, is the perfect format. No restrictions when it comes to length or brevity: a post can be a considered and meticulously composed 3,000-word essay, or a spurted splat of speculation or whimsy. No rules about structure or consistency of tone. A blogpost can be half-baked and barely proved: I feel zero responsibility to "do my research" before pontificating. Purely for my own pleasure, I do often go deep. But it's nearer the truth to say that some posts are outcomes of rambles across the archives of the internet, byproducts of the odd information trawled up and the lateral connections created.

Porém porém, vcs conhecem-nos. Casmurros & causas perdidas. Este vosso escriba vai recuperando a seu bel-prazer uma vinha que cortada, arrancada, deixada para morrer, imaginada despachada. Das raízes mais profundas surgiram umas quantas trepadeiras que não souberam desistir, fizemos-lhes cócegas, demos-lhes miminhos, e neste segundo ano temos uns quantos pés que se põem em bico a sonhar orgulho antigo, continuamos a descobrir novos brotos a levantar-se do chão, e não antecipámos as uvas que já tivemos num campo mais chato que plano meros meses antes. Editar na web indie em 2024 não é exercício muito diferente.
