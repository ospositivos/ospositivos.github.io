As ambições desmedidas da corporação do Alfabeto levam-na a tentar ambos: roubar os utilizadores dos outros e chular os seus. Que nos traz à segunda boa-má.

Google has spent the last 25 years trying to control the semantic web that it helped to build. Then it imposed this linguistic capitalism on the web at large via SEO: search engine optimization. The right word combinations brought you traffic, advertising, and money. Since then, Google has tried ever harder to become indispensable to the web – to become its OS. In particular, the company is obsessed with the "zero-click" outcome, where you get answers without having to leave Google. After raiding every public document the web has produced, Google now feels ready to dispense with the websites it has been indexing for 25 years. It intends to replace them with its own regurgitation machines. In any case, the company no longer needs us. Its contract with the human beings of the internet – traffic and links in exchange for advertising – is null and void.
