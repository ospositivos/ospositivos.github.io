Regressamos sempre ao local do crime. Imprensa, meio e mensagem, pistas à inteligência artificial em jornalismo, "how the medium of generative AI will impact our message". Recordando o impacto do (custo do) telegrafo para encurtar as longas peças opinadas cheias de floreados de finais do século XIX num novo tipo de escrita, mais conciso, neutro, "jornalístico",

technology and editorial are two sides of the same coin

This pattern, where technology acts as a prime-editor, has repeated itself through every technological revolution. Television brought us the anchorman. Search engines wrote countless boring headlines. And the wave of social sharing encouraged “These 10 headlines that you have to click to believe.” When video came to social media, auto-playing silent videos with text overlays suddenly became ubiquitous. This shift, driven by platform algorithms rather than a deliberate editorial strategy, marked the “pivot to video” era. Study the platform (the medium) and it will inform your editorial thrust.

Nota: estes artigos por um David Cohn espandem pelo tema noutras peças como "The CMS is your first editor" 9 jan 2017 (originalmente publicado no "nosso" A List Apart mas entretanto desaparecido?) ou um mais anterior "What is ‘Platform Intelligence’ — Embrace the constraints" 2 nov 2015. Investiguem.

À pergunta "What will be generative AI’s inevitable prime-editorial directive?", a possível direção avançada:

Although the jury is technically still out (...) the influence of generative AI on editorial will flatten the value of producing formats. Today, a lot of time, energy and talent are spent on formatting information in just the right way [ & ] the cost of production in terms of formatting could drop dramatically. I’m not suggesting the value-add of journalism disappears, but rather shifts from production toward something else. For example, in a world where formatting and production are greatly eased, the emphasis for journalists is to find the “new.” The journalist-as-writer could shift back to journalist-as-person-who-turns-over-rocks.

Ao novo, senhores! Ie, o regresso do velho: o jornalista que investiga & reporta, e talvez até com um cheirinho aos jornais do antigamente. Re-atalhado, de nada:

In the aftermath of decreased formatting costs would be a newfound emphasis on content that is provably human-created. If AI-created reach a fever pitch, we can expect to see a counterresurgence of in-person efforts. As cheap and dubious content spreads online, print may find a new value as a provably costly format (print production). Anything that can show the human touch could get new value, and that is the kind of editorial influence from generative AI that many folks who bleed black ink might welcome.
