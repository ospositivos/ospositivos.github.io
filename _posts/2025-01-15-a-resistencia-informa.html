Tendências do ano, cómicos para canto, agora os jornais, um outro pet peeve deste espaço.

Another big name is leaving The Washington Post. She resigned because “the Post, along with most mainstream news outlets, has failed spectacularly at a moment that we most need a robust, aggressive free press. Corporate and billionaire owners of major media outlets have betrayed their audiences’ loyalty and sabotaged journalism’s sacred mission — defending, protecting and advancing democracy."
in "The hits just keep on coming for The Washington Post" 14 jan 2025

No Poynter, 50 anos, "Journalism. Truth. Democracy." em mais um caso mediático de desertores que optam por abrir o seu próprio outlet noticioso. Caso específico com a tagline de "Not Owned By Anyone", jab ao Bezos, aposta de vários "fellow pro-democracy contrarians, many of whom have decamped from corporate media" em missão de vos trazer "fearless and distinctive reported opinion and cultural commentary without phony balance, euphemisms or gamified political punditry." Se fossemos nós a dizê-lo, era populismo. Anyhoos, antes tarde que nunca, uma semana antes faziam um levantamento de diversas outras iniciativas em linha (pun!), que podemos resumir num "employee ownership is not a new concept in journalism":

we wanted an organization that was run democratically by the people who were doing the work

Similar scenes have played out across the country in recent years as financially beleaguered media companies shed staff and workers chafe under controversial management decisions. Fed up with this instability, some journalists are starting their own outlets, ones in which they are in charge and can reach readers directly.
in "Why more and more journalists are launching worker-owned outlets" 8 jan 2025

Podem seguir da peça diversos exemplos pelos américas, nenhuma afinidade, por cá o Fumaça faz-nos caso:

The current wave of interest in worker ownership is likely driven by a desire to connect with news consumers. Worker-owned outlets can pitch themselves to their audiences as an organization that cares about giving them quality journalism, not extracting profits. [...] Workers at these outlets aren’t beholden to a rich owner or investor, but they also don’t have the money that comes from such sponsorship. "Nobody’s gonna get rich."
in "Why more and more journalists are launching worker-owned outlets" 8 jan 2025

Consigam navegar o ecossistema que sustenta "influencers" pelas redes sociais, e dessas redirecionar audiências a casa própria para que não dependam de qualquer algoritmo. Ganham pela proximidade, ganham porque os leitores anseiam informação descomprometida, especializada. Poderão estes sustentar dezenas de micro-redações nicho? Cremos que sim, as rotinas mediáticas favorecem a fragmentação, as fontes curadas, o "seguir" e "gostar" de personalidades, damos-lhe mais credibilidade do que estendemos a corporações. De resto, a longo prazo estaremos todos mortos, dizia John Maynard Keynes. Que um dia se agreguem novamente, por agora, descentralizai e multiplicai senhores, os tempos são de guerra.
