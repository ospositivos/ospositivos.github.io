Vamos fingir estupefação, segue-se recap dos últimos anos da tendência já aqui bramada ad nauseam:

I wish I could see a way out of the endless hype cycles that continue to elicit little more than cynicism from me

It’s perhaps hard to remember now, but in the early nineties, finding an audience really meant being discovered and highlighted by a small number of very rich publishing companies (or record labels, etc) who were most often not representative of their audiences. The web was a revolution: anyone could publish their words, their music, or their art, without asking anyone for permission, and they could find their communities equally permissionlessly.

The web, of course, didn’t turn out to be quite as utopian as the promise. Replacing publishers with Facebook wasn’t the original intention, but that’s what happened. And in the process, the power dynamics completely shifted. Over time, the centralized platforms disempowered their users by monopolizing more and more slices of everyday life that used to be free. The open, unlimited nature of the web that was originally used to distribute equity was now being used to suck it up and concentrate it in a handful of increasingly-wealthy people.

Which brings us to AI, the current hotness. The underlying purpose of AI is to allow wealth to access skill while removing from skill the ability to access wealth. This is the exact opposite of the power redistribution that led to so many people seeing such promise in the web. It’s very hard to get excited about technology that redistributes wealth and power in favor of people who already have it.
