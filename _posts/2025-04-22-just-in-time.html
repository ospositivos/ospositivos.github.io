Mais um coco de Ilan Manouach (“conceptual comics”) que não lemos, leremos, ou cremos em boa fé que se leiam, “puro desaire estético” do artista transmedia cujas intersecções em novas tecnologias já vos trouxemos diversas vezes antes contra adágio do artista que vos escreve: se não se lê, não é bd. Pondo o trans em media, expomos caso por domínios que nos ocupam e fazem parte da bagagem, mas para sempre estranhos à BD: web-cenas. Diz-nos Luke Wroblewski a propósito do motor de IA que desenvolveu no seu sítio:

Publishing has changed.

You don't see most of what I'm publishing these days and here's why. I’ve written 3 books, thousands of articles, and given hundreds of talks on digital product design. The generative AI feature on my Website searches all this content, finds, ranks, and re-ranks it in order to answer people’s questions on the topics I’ve written about. Because all my content has been broken down into almost atomic units, there’s an endless number of recombinations possible. Way more than I could have possibly ever written myself. Each corresponding answer is a unique composition of content that did not exist before. Every response is created for a specific person with a specific need at a specific time. If you keep adding bits of additional content to an overall corpus for generative AI to draw from, the responses can remain timely and relevant. For me this feels like the new way of publishing. I'm building a corpus with infinite malleability instead of a more limited number of discrete artifacts.
