Misturamos texto atalhado à vossa pressa (toil) e nossa conveniência (labor) porque há desleixos que não o são e se nenhum outro sentido, sintam-se dessa. Outros há, porém, que nem por isso:

Slop emerges when we eliminate not just toil (the burdensome aspects of work) but labor itself (the meaningful human engagement with creation) [que] makes the very act of creating something of meaning almost irrelevant.
in "A Tool That Crushes Creativity" 20 out 2025

Quem escreve poderá interessar ou não, importa o que se diz, como o lêem conta mais, o que lhe fazem arruma história. Concluímos da internet zombie com o advento de feeds infinitos de conteúdos artificiais que se consomem sem reação, obrigando-nos retocar abrangência do "não são comics se ninguém os lê" que enunciámos de eras mais analógicas à vasta maioria de conteúdos online: não lhe temos pó como comicha artistas, focados no acto de criação e contemplação per se —porque, artsys—, mas punx de vocação insistimos questionar o uso subsequente. Os feeds de maquinação aleatória destituidos do esforço de criação não nos são inúteis porque os conseguimos desmascarar desprovidos de agência humana na sua génese, são-nos inúteis porque essa agência se esgota no consumo passivo-massivo anestesiado-programado. A descrição que se segue é das mais aterradoras que vos podemos trazer, já em scroll num ecrã na vossa mão:

Think too long, and it all begins to feel sinister.

Synthetic content is not exactly new, but lately it has become a load-bearing part of the internet. Beginning around November 2024, the internet experienced a slop tipping point, in which the quantity of AI-generated articles being published on the web surpassed the quantity of articles written by humans.

Social-media companies have succeeded in connecting the world and have become so massive and so messily human that AI slop created by the proprietary LLMs fills a need. Imagine a social network in which, instead of third-party links or incendiary political posts, the atomic unit of content is not text at all but a universal language of eminently consumable short-form video, to be remixed and traded back and forth between users who are soft-brain scrolling from the toilet.

Each video, on its own, is a digital curio, the value of which drops to zero after the initial view. In aggregate, they take on an overwhelming, almost narcotic effect. They are contextless, stupefying, and, most important, never-ending. Each successive clip is both effortlessly consumable and wholly unsatisfying.

All of this exacts a fuzzy psychological toll. Spend enough time online, and you will see that not only is this cheaply rendered synthetic content everywhere; it is quietly shaping culture. It’s become a way that marketers advertise, that politicians produce propaganda. It’s changing how people communicate with one another. Our brains are being sous-vided in machine-made engagement.
in "A Tool That Crushes Creativity" 20 out 2025
