this is what it means to shift risk

Workers and corporations seek to "de-risk" their position. Workers can vote for politicians who will set minimum wages, punish unsafe working conditions and on-the-job harassment, and require health and disability insurance. They can also unionize and get some or all of these measures through collective bargaining.

Bosses also strive to de-risk their position, by shifting the risk onto workers. One of the most profound, effective and pervasive sites of de-risking is the gig economy, in which workers are not guaranteed any wages. That's what bosses mean by a "flexible workforce": a workforce that can coerced into assuming risk that properly belongs to its employers. By deploying the legal fiction of worker misclassification in which an employee is classed as an "independent contractor," the boss can shift all the risk of misallocating labor onto workers [...] even if the pay and working conditions are inadequate. The rise and rise of a massive "flexible workforce" is actually the rise and rise of a system in which labor assumes capital's risk.
