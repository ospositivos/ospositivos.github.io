Do mais recente livro de Tim Berners-Lee ("This Is For Everyone" 2025) que não lemos, mas lemos resumo que vos resumimos — OS POSITIVOS, três graus de separação em leituras contrariadas porque o vosso tempo é escasso e a nossa má-vontade infinita:

The philosophy espoused in the book is that the internet should not be a tool for the concentration of power among an elite. He wants the internet to function in a way that maximises the benefit to society.

Cada qual permanece na posse dos seus próprios dados ("a radically different approach to what we have today where big tech companies own most of it"), e propõe duas hipóteses: intervenção estatal à regulação da big tech e "perhaps, he contends, people themselves can begin building better alternatives." Destas, descentralizar, descentralizar, descentralizar.

A key part of the problem is that we become tied into platforms run by the giants. Owning our data would go some way to having a fairer relationship. Instead of being locked into an increasingly small number of big tech firms this would open the door to new platforms offering a better deal.

O Sir Berners-Lee tem aludido ao tema com maior antecedência que muitos porque a sua relação à WWW é privilegiada, um recap de artigos na Wired desde o "The Father of the Web" 1997 exempla-vos evolução:

  • 2003: Web 'Shaman' Fights His Demons
  • 2004: Tim Berners-Lee: Can't We All Just Get Along?
  • 2007: Sir Tim Berners-Lee: Net Neutrality Good; Lock-in Bad.
  • 2012: Tim Berners-Lee Takes the Stand to Keep the Web Free
  • 2014: Tim Berners-Lee: we need to re-decentralise the web
  • 2017: The battle for the soul of the internet has well and truly begun
  • 2018: Tim Berners-Lee wants to save the web with decentralised data storage
  • 2019: How Tim Berners-Lee plans to fix the web
