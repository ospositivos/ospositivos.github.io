blatant liars are drawing political support around the world
it seems we collectively have ceased to care about the truth

Leitura apressada entre paragens por feeds acumulados e nenhum vagar por queimar — acreditem-nos, pegar-lhe-íamos fogo —, trazemos-vos mashup descartado de espiritualidades a transcender factos (pun!) e reimaginado do conhecimento que nos temos, autenticidade:

A truly human life demands not only knowledge of facts about reality, but also a subjective understanding of the world and of one’s place in it. This is because truth deals in facts while meanings deal in interpretations.

Understanding, unlike knowledge, is a matter of how we look at the world, of our thinking habits and of cultural constructs – chiefly identities, values and institutions. These things fulfil their function of making us feel at home in the world without making any claim to truth.

The insistence on truth and devaluation of meaning has led to [...] a sense of alienation – a breaking of social, historical and traditional bonds with each other and with ourselves. This alienation has provided a feeding ground for populists and anti-democrats, who present themselves as a corrective to the crisis of meaning.

The champions of democratic truth would do well to remember that the world democracy tries to build is a world of meaningful human endeavour, not just dry knowledge and fact-finding.
in "Is democracy always about truth? Why we may need to loosen our views to heal our divisions" 23 dez 2025

Continuaremos esse empreendimento significativo de-e-para o colectivo, mesmo que o tenhamos de alienar de todas as vezes.
