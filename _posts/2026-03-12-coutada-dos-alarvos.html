I want to make it easier to put that into words. To be able to say, if someone says “check out this new AI thing!”: naw, it’s cool, I’m vegetarian. Generative AI vegetarianism, simply put, is avoiding generative AI tools as much as you can in your day-to-day life. For me, that means [...] choosing software products that don’t have AI features, and supporting companies and organizations and creators that deliberately avoid generative AI tools. [...] And to hope that, over time, some subset of companies and organizations will lean into that, and make deliberately AI-free books and music and videos and video games.

Como o que não como, a política da meat na IA não é inconsequente ao mundo e todos neste. Apesar de varrido para longe de vista que não estrague apetites, não surge cozinhado no ecrã por magia e pós de perlimpimpim. Da mesma página de onde retirámos citação — saltamos o rippanço de conteúdos alheios que damos por adquirido até entre os mais desatentos — atalhamos:

Generative AI tools shift more power to the already powerful. • Generative AI tools are easily used as an “accountability sink”: harmful decisions can be made while obscuring the human decision-makers behind them. • Generative AI tools have a dial for bias, discrimination, and racism that you can’t control. • More precarious employment as the result of companies adopting AI tools has already led to worse working environments and widespread layoffs. • The process for creating and improving generative AI tools incentivizes restricting rather than sharing information.

Somando paralelos a indústrias que consomem recursos acima dos sustentáveis pelo planeta, preferimos alternativas sem crueldade e cruzamos à sinopse do "The Sexual Politics of Meat: A Feminist-Vegan Critical Theory" (1990) que nunca lemos mas ecoa argumentos sobejamente conhecidos entre degenerados da nossa liça:

Behind every meal of meat is an absence: the death of the animal whose place the meat takes. The absent referent functions to cloak the violence inherent to meat eating, to protect the conscience of the meat eater and render the idea of individual animals as immaterial to anyone’s selfish desires. The function of the absent referent is to keep our "meat" separated from being seen as having been someone, to allow for the moral abandonment of another being.
