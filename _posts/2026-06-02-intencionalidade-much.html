Ie, as "intenções dos humanos por detrás do computador" in Real Nós. Cory D. atira hoje à arena algumas linhas que abreviamos à vossa atenção, seguindo dizeres de Mark Fisher no "seeming of an intent without an intender as eerie", complementando um "art is everything you don't have to do" com o "art is intended to make other people feel something - this distinguishes art from beauty". Feiura então:

This distinction has lately become far more significant, thanks to the rise of images and words that have the seeming of intent, but who don't have an intender. We ended up hallucinating an intent. When you prompt an AI to generate words or pixels, you are conveying some intent about the feeling you want the people who experience the model's output to experience. The problem is that the AI doesn't have any intent of its own – it just has statistical predictions.

Sem desmérito da restante tese, a distinção a fazer encontra-se já na própria enunciação acima: intenção da máquina, intenção do maquinista. Admitindo que a máquina não tem nenhuma — e senhores, admitimos nada, não descurem o bias da big tech por detrás dessas, digam-me se já ouviram esta antes: capitalismo!—, recuperamos dislikes anteriores do C. Doctorow para nuances, pequenas-pequenas nuances que acumuladas denotam vontades explícitas:

The more you work with the AI, the more art-like its output becomes. If the AI generates 50 variations from your prompt and you choose one, that's one more microdecision infused into the work. If you re-prompt and re-re-prompt the AI to generate refinements, then each of those prompts is a new payload of microdecisions that the AI can spread out across all the words of pixels, increasing the amount of communicative intent in each one.
